🌀 Cooling Fan Policy Simulation

This prescriptive analytics project evaluates two replacement strategies for a three-fan cooling system in a critical data center rack:

  • Current Policy: Replace fans individually when they fail.
  • Proposed Policy: Replace all three fans whenever one fails.

Using Monte Carlo simulation, the team implemented both policies in Excel and Python. The simulation considered cost parameters for:

  • Fan purchase ($32/unit)
  • Technician labour ($30/hour)
  • Server downtime ($10/minute)

🔍 Results Summary

  • Total cost (Current): $21,380
  • Total cost (Proposed): $34,770
  • Total operating time: 20,532.5 hrs vs 51,249.25 hrs
  • Cost per hour: $1.041 vs $0.678

The proposed policy, despite higher costs, leads to better cost-efficiency and uptime.

🧠 Key Takeaway

Prescriptive analytics enabled data-driven decision-making, demonstrating that a proactive replacement strategy reduces long-term cost per hour and improves operational resilience.
